## Case Study : Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look genuine.

For example, a student named Ankit received an email that appeared to be from his bank. The message said that his account would be blocked if he didn’t verify his details immediately. It also included a link that looked official. Without thinking much, Ankit clicked the link and entered his login details on the website. However, the website was fake, and his information was sent directly to the attacker.

This type of attack generally follows a pattern. First, the scammer sends a message that looks real and creates urgency. Next, the victim is asked to click on a link or download something. Then, they are directed to a fake website where they enter their personal details. Finally, the attacker uses this information to access accounts or steal money.

Phishing mostly targets students, working professionals, and elderly people who may not carefully check the authenticity of such messages. The consequences can include loss of money, hacked accounts, and misuse of personal information. 

This case highlights the importance of being cautions online and verifying suspicious message before taking any action.

## Fictional Scenario
----

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pretend to be trusted individuals or organizations to steal sensitive information like bank details, passwords, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

A real incident reported in India involved a real estate company in Pune that lost around ₹40 lakh due to a phishing attack. In this case, cybercriminals impersonated the company’s director and contacted an employee. They created a sense of urgency and instructed the employee to transfer money to a specific bank account. Believing the request to be genuine, the employee transferred a large amount, which later turned out to be fraudulent.

This type of crime typically happens in steps. First, the attacker pretends to be a trusted person (like a boss or bank official). Next, they contact the victim through email or message. Then, they create urgency or pressure so the victim acts quickly. Finally, the victim shares sensitive information or transfers money, leading to financial loss.

Phishing mainly targets employees, students, and individuals who may not verify such requests carefully. The consequences include loss of money, data theft, and mental stress.

This case shows how important it is to verify messages and not trust urgent financial requests without proper confirmation.
